# Ga'eš

**Ga'eš** (sumerian: ga-eški and ga-eš5ki; also known as **Karzida**) was an ancient Sumerian city closely associated with the city of [[Ur]]. Based on archaeological evidence, it is proposed to be located at the site of modern [[Tell Sakhariya]], approximately 6.45 km northeast of [[Ur]] in present-day [[Iraq]].

## Religious Significance

Ga'eš was the site of the **Akiti festival** of [[Nanna]] (the moon god) and his consort [[Ningal]]. The festival was held every year for 11 days in the seventh month and 7 days in the first month of the year. The festival began at Nanna's temple in [[Ur]] and ended in Ga'eš, possibly traveling via a canal.

The temple of Nanna/Sin at Ga'eš was called the **Karzida** (kar-zi-da). The names Karzida and Ga'eš appear to have been used interchangeably for the city.

## Historical References

### Year Names

Several year names from the [[Third Dynasty of Ur]] reference Ga'eš:
- The 36th year name of [[Shulgi]]: "Year Nanna of Ga'eš was brought into his temple"
- The 9th year name of [[Amar-Sin]]: "Year En-Nanna-Amar-Sin-kiagra, was installed for the third time as en-priestess of Nanna of Ga'eš / of Karzida"

### Enheduanna's Temple Hymns

One of the [[Temple Hymns of Enheduanna]], the daughter of [[Sargon of Akkad]], is dedicated to Ga'eš and the Karzida temple:

> "Shrine, great sanctuary?, founded at a cattle-pen, 'Small' city, ... of Suen Karzida, your interior is a ... place, your foundation is holy and clean... O Karzida, he, Ašimbabbar, has placed the house upon your ... has taken his place on your dais. The house of Nanna in Ga'eš."

### Lament for Sumer and Ur

Ga'eš is also mentioned in the *Lament for Sumer and Ur*:

> "They destroyed Gaeš like milk poured out to dogs, and shattered its finely fashioned statues. 'Alas, the destroyed city, my destroyed house,' Its sacred Ĝipar of en priesthood was defiled."

### Wrestling Competitions

From tablets found at [[Ur]], it is known that wrestling competitions were held at Ga'eš, with texts mentioning "the 'house of wrestling' in the Akiti (building), issued in Ga'eš, during the Akiti month."

## Duration of Occupation

Ga'eš appears in texts from the [[Early Dynastic II period]] through the [[Isin-Larsa period]]. The final textual mention dates from the time of [[Larsa]] ruler [[Sin-Iddinam]] (c. 1849–1843 BC).

## Priesthood

The en-priestess of Nanna served at Ga'eš/Karzida. [[Amar-Sin]] established a Giparu (nunnery) for the en-priestess there. When the en-priestess died, she was buried with a "golden crown (aga), which is followed by five other golden objects."
