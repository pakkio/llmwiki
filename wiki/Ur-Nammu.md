# Ur-Nammu

**Ur-Nammu** (Sumerian: 𒌨𒀭𒇉; c. 2112–2094 BC) was the founder of the [[Third Dynasty of Ur]] and the first king of the [[Neo-Sumerian Empire]], ruling in the 21st century BC. He was a major builder, lawgiver, and military leader.

## Rise to Power

After the decline of the [[Akkadian Empire]], the [[Gutians]] controlled much of Mesopotamia. [[Utu-hengal]], a king of [[Uruk]], drove the Gutians out of southern [[Babylonia]]. Upon Utu-hengal's death, his governor of [[Ur]], Ur-Nammu, moved the kingship to the city.

## Reign and Achievements

### Building Projects

Ur-Nammu built many temples, including the famous [[Ziggurat of Ur]], which contained the shrine of [[Nanna]], the moon god. Agriculture was improved through irrigation projects.

### The Code of Ur-Nammu

Ur-Nammu issued the **Code of Ur-Nammu**, one of the oldest known law codes in world history. A fragment of this code was identified in Istanbul in 1952. It predates the [[Code of Hammurabi]] by approximately 300 years.

### Deification and Afterlife

Ur-Nammu and his successor [[Shulgi]] were both deified during their reigns. After his death, Ur-Nammu continued as a hero-figure: one of the surviving works of [[Sumerian literature]] describes the death of Ur-Nammu and his journey to the underworld.

## Inscribed Artifacts

An inscribed brick of Ur-Nammu was found at [[Tell Sakhariya]], describing "the construction of a barag – a pedestal or podium and a garden." Stamped bricks from his reign are also found at the Ziggurat of Ur.
