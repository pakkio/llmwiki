# Tell Sakhariya

**Tell Sakhariya** (also known as Tell Abu Ba'arura Shimal) is an archaeological site located about 6.45 kilometers northeast of the ancient city of [[Ur]] in present-day [[Iraq]]. It has been proposed as the location of the ancient city of **[[Ga'eš]]**.

## Location and Description

The site lies at coordinates 30° 58' 33.84" N, 46° 08' 28.36" E, approximately 6.45 km northeast of [[Ur]]. It measures about 250 meters by 200 meters with two high points about 3.5 meters above the plain, separated by a saddle. The ziggurat at [[Ur]] can be seen from the summit of the site.

## Excavation History

The site was first noted during an archaeological survey of the region in the 1960s as a [[Kassite]] period occupation. It was excavated from December 2011 to January 2012 by a joint team from the [[Iraqi State Board of Antiquities and Heritage]] and the [[State University of New York at Stony Brook]], led by Elizabeth Stone and Paul Zimansky.

## Findings

Seven trenches were dug. The excavation revealed three occupational levels:
- **Top layer**: [[Kassite]] pottery fragments, a late Kassite kiln, and late Kassite burials
- **Second layer**: [[Sealand Dynasty]] ceramics with lithic, metal, floral, and faunal remains (primarily cattle, sheep, and goats)
- **Final (earliest) layer**: A very large mud or clay platform made from clean material

Key finds included:
- An inscribed brick of [[Ur-Nammu]] (first ruler of the [[Third Dynasty of Ur]]) describing the construction of a barag (pedestal/podium) and a garden
- Four fragmentary inscribed bricks
- Three inscribed cones (one datable to [[Larsa]] ruler [[Rim-Sîn I]])
- Two Sumerian language [[cuneiform tablets]] (one early Kassite, one dated to year 28 of [[Shulgi]])

## Identification as Ga'eš

Based on the archaeology, Tell Sakhariya has been proposed as the site of the Ur III period city of **[[Ga'eš]]** (also spelled Gaeš), site of the Akiti festival of [[Nanna]]/Sin. After the excavation season, a nearby prison was expanded by the Iraqi government, blocking access to the site and precluding further campaigns.
